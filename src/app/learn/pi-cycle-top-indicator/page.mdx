# Pi Cycle Top Indicator: How Two Moving Averages Called Every Bitcoin Peak

*By Jay, Bitcoin investor since 2017* · March 2026

Of all the on-chain and technical indicators used to identify Bitcoin cycle tops, the Pi Cycle Top Indicator has the most striking track record. It has signaled the peak of every major Bitcoin bull market — sometimes within days of the exact top. Understanding how it works, why it works, and where it fails is essential for any cycle-aware investor.

## The Math

The Pi Cycle Top Indicator uses two simple moving averages:

- The **111-day simple moving average (SMA)** of Bitcoin's price
- **Twice the 350-day simple moving average** (350-day SMA × 2) of Bitcoin's price

The indicator signals a potential cycle top when the 111-day SMA crosses above the 350-day SMA × 2. The name "Pi Cycle" comes from the observation that 350 divided by 111 equals approximately 3.153 — remarkably close to the mathematical constant pi (3.14159). Whether this is coincidence or reflects some deeper market periodicity is debated, but the correlation has held across multiple cycles.

## Historical Accuracy

**2013 Peak (November 29, 2013 — $1,163)**
The 111-day SMA crossed above 2× the 350-day SMA within two days of the absolute top. Bitcoin proceeded to lose more than 80% of its value over the following year.

**2017 Peak (December 17, 2017 — $19,783)**
The crossover occurred on December 17, 2017 — the exact day Bitcoin hit its all-time high before crashing to $3,200 over the next 12 months.

**2021 Peak (April 12, 2021 — $64,863)**
The crossover signaled on April 13, 2021, one day after Bitcoin's local top of ~$64,800. While Bitcoin later reached a higher nominal peak in November 2021 ($69,000), the April signal marked the end of the first parabolic move and the beginning of a more volatile, distribution-heavy market structure.

Three consecutive cycle tops, each signaled within days. No other indicator has matched this consistency.

## Why It Works

The 111-day SMA captures medium-term momentum. The 350-day SMA represents long-term trend. In a sustained bull market, the 111-day average accelerates faster than the 350-day average because it is more responsive to recent price action.

When the faster average overtakes twice the slower average, it means short-term prices have run so far ahead of the long-term trend that the market is statistically extended. At that point, historically, the market has exhausted its upside momentum and reversed.

The indicator works because it captures the mathematical structure of parabolic exhaustion — the point at which acceleration can no longer be sustained.

## Limitations

**Only signals tops, not bottoms.** The Pi Cycle Top is a peak detector. It provides no information about when the bottom has been reached or when accumulation is optimal.

**Small sample size.** Three correct signals is remarkable, but statistically insufficient to call it proven. Bitcoin has only existed through four halving cycles total.

**2021 nuance.** The April 2021 signal was technically correct (it marked a major peak), but Bitcoin later exceeded that price in November 2021. Depending on how you define "the top," the signal was either perfect or slightly early.

**Not actionable as a sole signal.** Even if the indicator fires, selling at the exact moment of the crossover has historically been optimal only in retrospect. Markets can remain extended for days or weeks. The Pi Cycle Top is best used in combination with other indicators — exactly how BitcoinCycle Clock presents it.

## Practical Application

The Pi Cycle Top is most useful as a temperature check:

- **Ratio below 0.7** — You are likely in the early or mid stages of a cycle. Historically, this has not been a period of elevated topping risk.
- **Ratio 0.8–0.95** — The market is warming. This is a zone where taking some profit or reducing leverage becomes prudent from a risk-management perspective.
- **Ratio above 0.95** — Historical danger zone. Not a guarantee of a top, but a signal that has preceded every top in Bitcoin's history. Maximum caution warranted.

## Key Takeaways

- The Pi Cycle Top Indicator has signaled every Bitcoin cycle peak since 2013, typically within days of the exact top.
- It measures the convergence of medium-term and long-term momentum, capturing the mathematical signature of parabolic exhaustion.
- It only signals tops — not bottoms, not mid-cycle corrections, not accumulation zones.
- Three correct signals is remarkable but not statistically conclusive. Use it alongside MVRV, Puell Multiple, and other indicators for a more complete picture.

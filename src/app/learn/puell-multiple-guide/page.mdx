# Puell Multiple: Reading Miner Stress to Time the Bitcoin Market

*By Jay, Bitcoin investor since 2017* · March 2026

Miners are the only participants in the Bitcoin ecosystem who must sell. They have electricity bills, hardware costs, and facility expenses that cannot be paid in Bitcoin. This structural selling pressure makes miner economics one of the most informative signals for identifying cycle extremes. The Puell Multiple captures this signal in a single number.

## What It Measures

The Puell Multiple compares the daily USD value of newly issued Bitcoin to the 365-day moving average of that same value:

**Puell Multiple = Daily Issuance Value (USD) / 365-Day Average of Daily Issuance Value (USD)**

When the Multiple is high, miners are earning far more than their annual average. When it is low, they are earning far less. These extremes correspond to predictable market behavior.

## Why Miner Behavior Matters

Miners are forced sellers — they must convert some portion of their Bitcoin revenue into fiat currency to cover operating costs. This creates consistent sell-side pressure in the market. The amount of that pressure fluctuates based on two variables:

1. **Bitcoin price** — when BTC price is high, daily issuance value is high, and miners have more to sell.
2. **Block reward** — after each halving, the number of new coins issued per block drops by 50%.

The interplay between these two variables creates predictable patterns. After a halving, daily issuance value drops mechanically (same price × fewer coins = lower revenue). During bull markets, price appreciation more than compensates for the reduced block reward, pushing daily issuance value to new highs.

## Historical Readings

### Cycle Tops (Puell Multiple > 4)

| Date | Puell Multiple | BTC Price | What Happened Next |
|------|---------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Nov 2013 | ~6.0 | ~$1,163 | 85% crash over 12 months |
| Dec 2017 | ~5.2 | ~$19,783 | 84% crash over 12 months |
| Apr 2021 | ~4.1 | ~$64,800 | Local top, 50% correction |

When the Puell Multiple exceeds 4, miners are earning more than four times their annual average. Historically, this level of excess revenue has coincided with cycle peaks. Miners are incentivized to sell aggressively at these elevated levels, flooding the market with supply at exactly the moment retail buyers are most eager to purchase.

### Cycle Bottoms (Puell Multiple < 0.5)

| Date | Puell Multiple | BTC Price | What Happened Next |
|------|---------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Jan 2015 | ~0.3 | ~$200 | 9,800% gain to 2017 peak |
| Dec 2018 | ~0.3 | ~$3,200 | 2,050% gain to 2021 peak |
| Nov 2022 | ~0.4 | ~$16,000 | Recovery to $64,000+ |

When the Puell Multiple drops below 0.5, miners are earning less than half their annual average. This represents miner stress — many miners are operating at a loss. Weak miners capitulate (shut down or sell reserves), creating a wash-out effect that historically marks the final phase of a bear market.

## The Post-Halving Puell Reset

Each halving mechanically resets the Puell Multiple downward. The block reward is cut by 50%, which immediately halves the numerator (daily issuance value) while the denominator (365-day average) still reflects pre-halving levels. This creates a period of artificially low readings for several months after each halving, regardless of price action.

This is important to understand because a low Puell Multiple shortly after a halving does not necessarily signal a buying opportunity — it may simply reflect the mechanical effect of the reward reduction. The indicator becomes most useful 6–12 months after a halving, once the 365-day average has adjusted to the new issuance rate.

## Limitations

**Lagging during transitions.** Because the denominator is a 365-day average, the Puell Multiple is slow to adjust after halvings and after sharp price movements. During rapid price changes, the indicator can give misleading readings for weeks.

**Mining efficiency changes.** Modern mining operations are far more efficient and better capitalized than those of 2013 or 2017. Miners today may tolerate lower Puell Multiple readings without capitulating, potentially shifting the historically reliable bottom zone lower over time.

**Derivatives and hedging.** Large mining operations now use futures, options, and forward contracts to hedge their revenue. This reduces the direct relationship between daily issuance value and actual selling pressure in spot markets.

## Practical Application

- **Puell below 0.5 + MVRV below 0** — Strong historical signal of a cycle bottom. This combination has preceded every major bull market.
- **Puell above 4 + Pi Cycle ratio above 0.95** — Strong historical signal of a cycle top. Multiple indicators confirming each other increases confidence.
- **Puell rising steadily** — Bull market in progress. As long as the Multiple is between 1.0 and 3.0, conditions are historically favorable for continued appreciation.
- **Puell declining from high levels** — Distribution may be underway. Watch for confirmation from other indicators before drawing conclusions.

## Key Takeaways

- The Puell Multiple captures miner selling pressure, one of the most structural forces in the Bitcoin market.
- Readings above 4 have historically coincided with cycle peaks. Readings below 0.5 have coincided with bottoms.
- Post-halving readings are mechanically depressed — wait 6–12 months for the indicator to normalize.
- Use the Puell Multiple alongside MVRV and Pi Cycle for a multi-factor view of cycle position.
